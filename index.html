Why use DotcomSeekr?
A small tool for people who think in domain names first.
Idea-driven search
Start from a keyword, not a registrar sales page.
  • Enter a single base keyword (or root brand idea).
  • Generate and check variants across a set of TLDs.
  • Stay focused on names that actually fit your brand vision.
Scheduled checks
Set it once, let it quietly watch in the background.
  • Choose an interval (e.g. every 60 minutes, 6 hours, or 24 hours).
  • Each run logs availability snapshots to a local file.
  • You decide when to export and act — no noisy notifications.
Results you can use
Export, filter, and share without wrestling a UI.
  • CSV and JSON outputs for spreadsheet people and builders.
  • Optional email summary if you prefer a daily “what changed?” digest.
  • Keep a permanent history of when domains were seen free or taken.
How it fits your workflow
Designed for founders who bookmark domains as a hobby.
  • 1
    Add a base keyword and TLDs.
    e.g. “butternutbaby” with .com, .co, .net. That’s your core naming hypothesis — DotcomSeekr just watches it.
  • 2
    Choose your check interval.
    Start with hourly checks while you’re actively naming, then taper down to every 6–24 hours once you’re just waiting for something to free up.
  • 3
    Let it build an availability history.
    Each run records whether the domain is available. Over time that creates a simple, queryable log of “when could I have taken this?”.
  • 4
    Export when you’re ready to decide.
    Filter by “first seen available” dates, sort by TLD, and move the best candidates into your registrar of choice when you’re ready to buy.
Roadmap glimpse
From desktop utility to full search platform.

The current build is a deliberately small Windows app — a single-purpose tool for idea-first domain checking. Next up:

  • Multi-keyword collections (e.g. product lines, sub-brands).
  • Expired / dropping domain discovery toggle.
  • API integrations with registrars for one-click check-and-buy.
